Wright + Entrepreneurship + Everything In Between

Stepping Up and How Doing So Further Fueled My Entrepreneurshial Mindset

Learning

Adobe Xd

During Nu School's Product Development conference, we were advised to have a minimum viable product (MVP) to demo for our pitch. To create the most efficient MVP possible, I thought it would be best to create UX mockups and wireframe for how Wright's website would work. Through online research I came across a software called Adobe Xd, the most popular tool for UX and wireframe creation.

With my background in computer science and eagerness to learn new technology skills, I committed to self-learning Adobe Xd on my own, though extensive research. This way my teammates could focus in on the strengths they can bring to Wright’s development.

While self-learning a brand development software, mere weeks before pitch day seemed daunting, I knew that as an entrepreneur it’s crucial to step up to face challenges for the sake of both your team and company.

Through extensive readings of Xd documentation and reaching out online to UX professionals in the industry for help, I had multiple mockups and wireframes ready. I showcased the initial designs to the Wright team to get their input. Eventually we settled on a sleek, minimalistic look. A look that was praised by the rest of the Nu School fellows, and sponsoring entrepreneur

A New

Passion...

After Nu School 2020 had ended, I knew I wanted to pick up UX development as a hobby, both to further improve Wright as well as to strengthen my skillset for future entrepreneurship ventures. My newfound passion led me to develop UX mockups and wireframes for a new concept: letsshoot.com, a networking website for artists and clients alike.

Since Nu School, I've been completing Udacity's "Human-Computer Interaction course. It's a 16-week course that details design principles, and how UX best practices can create innovative technologies in education. healthcare, other industries.

...And a New

Position!

My developing love for UX as a hobby led me to network with multiple UX professionals on LinkedIn. Seeing firsthand how they were aiding in the process of developing brand new products, many of them aimed at solving social issues like Wright further ignited my passion for entrepreneurship.

At the end of August, mere weeks after Wright's Nu School pitch, I connected with Dr. Tracy Flood, a medical researcher, and entrepreneur. Dr. Flood was in the process of developing a startup known as Broadstreet, a data analysis company that creates free-to-use infographics detailing COVID-19 trends in different communities across the United States. Broadstreet was looking for UX Design Interns to join their team. After hearing about the hard work Broadstreet was doing in aiding people's understanding of the pandemic, I was eager to join the startup. I applied for the UX Design Internship and shared my resume and portfolio with Dr. Flood and was promptly hired!

Currently, I'm working with a team to use Adobe Xd to develop a prototype of how Broadstreet's new website should look to better showcase current projects the Broadstreet team is working on. 

Combining

the Puzzle

Pieces

Getting the chance to learn Adobe Xd to further my own entrepreneurship venture with Wright, and getting to use these skills to help contribute to a new startup like Broadstreet has given me the opportunity to pursue entrepreneurial ventures that I otherwise may not have known about.
