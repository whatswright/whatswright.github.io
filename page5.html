Updates to Wright:

[COMPLETED] Deemphasized Focus on Coursework: 
Originally, Wright was meant to provide students the option to take free writing courses to improve their skills, alongside a place to write well-crafted takes on current events. After feedback from Nu School, the team decided to focus more on the latter to have a more focused mission for future users to get behind.

Other companies such as Khan Academy, Udemy, and Coursera provide free writing programs similar to what we wanted to provide. The main difference, however, was that Wright would be "major oriented" so that students could take writing courses dedicated to specific fields (i.e STEM, Liberal Arts, Business, etc.) We still feel like this is a worthwhile goal to pursue, but will put further effort into making it a reality if/when Wright becomes more popular and has a consistent user base.

[Completed] New Slogan: 
On August 24th, 2020 Wright received a new slogan! "Let Your Opinions Take Flight!"

While an admittingly small update, this new slogan emphasized our focus on creating a space for users to voice their opinions in an articulated way and see opposing sides to a worldwide issue. The slogan also serves as a fun nod to The Wright Brothers. While Wright isn't named after them (it's a double entendre of the amalgamation of Write+Right and the verb for "building something") it rolls off the tongue quite well!   

[In Progress] Live Beta Launch: 
Through free HTML, CSS, and JavaScript coursework at FreeCodeCamp.org, topped with a handful of YouTube tutorials, we are hoping to have a beta release of Wright.com (pending URL name) available for users to test. The beta will be based on the already completed UX mockups I completed during Nu School.
 
Thanks to undergraduate classes such as Computer Networks, I have working familiarity in creating a real-time chat service for users to communicate with one another in Java using Socket IO. This will aid in creating the social aspect of Wright that the team planned out, where users can ask for help and advice on their writing pieces. 

The estimated launch for the beta is February 2021, to give the team all of Winter Break and more to hone in on completion.

[In Progress] Social Media Campaign:
The goal for the beta isn't to get feedback just from our friends and family. We want Wright to be known to people all around the world. That's why by January 2021, assuming development on the beta is on schedule, we hope to promote Wright's beta through YouTube, TikTok, Twitter, and Reddit to get the word out. 30 - 60-second promotional content will be created using Final Cut Pro X to be released on all social media platforms.

Using screengrabs of our wireframe and mockups, the team has completed our first promotional ad for Wright. However, to truthfully advertise the full experience that users will have when they test out our day one beta, we'd like to incorporate footage of that too, and show users where we want Wright to be headed design-wise.

We're hoping that we can collect sample data on people's likes, dislikes, and general comments of their time using their beta.
