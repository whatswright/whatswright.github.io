Timeline of Wright

June 2020: "Due to Covid-19, this Internship program will NOT be moving forward this summer.

"Now what am I going to do this summer?!"

It was the summer before my senior year, I HAD to make this one count. 

July 2020: "Anyone have their internship cancelled? Consider helping me start Nu School!" - Daniel A. (CEO of Persist IQ)

One LinkedIn Post and application later I became a Tech-Fellow at Nu School, a startup created by 1 CEO + over 600 fellow students to give college students worldwide the oppurtunity to network, and create a minimum viable product (MVP) to pitch to a group of Y Combinator entrepreneurs  by August.

July - August 2020: Chicago, New York City, and Varanasi

Through online networking I met two new friends, turned business partners, Elizabeth and Dirghayu. Despite being from New York City and Varanasi, the two of us had something deeply in common: a desire to pitch a startup that solves a social issue in an accessible way. That's how we settled on Wright! My role for Wright was to develop UX mockups and a wireframe for how Wright's website would work (neighter of which I've ever done before!)

July - August 2020: Conferences, Development, Conferences (and repeat)

Now that we had a team formed, Elizabeth, Dirghayu, and I balanced our weeks by attending conferences on Product Development, Design Thinking, and Finance held by Y Combinator backed entrepreneurs, and personal Zoom conferences to keep track of our progress. 

August 2020: Pitch Day!

The day was finally here! Each Nu School school fellow  was tasked with pitching one public pitch for peer review, and one "second-draft" private pitch to give to Daniel. After giving/receieving feedback Nu School 2020 came to an end! 

September 2020 - Future: Beyond Nu School... Wright Lives On!

Even though Nu School 2020 is over, work on Wright still isn't done! Since August, the team and I have been hard at work balancing both school and further developing Wright. We hope to keep everyone updated!
