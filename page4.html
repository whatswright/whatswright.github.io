The Factor that Led to Wright's Development

Nu School

Nu School is a dual internship/business education platform pitched by Daniel Ahmadizadeh, the CEO of the Y Combinator-backed marketing company PersistIQ. According to Daniel, Nu School is for students who had their internships canceled because of COVID-19 to develop startup ideas, create a minimum viable product in teams, learn to give a successful pitch, and attend conferences held by his friends and colleagues from the Y Combinator.

As part of the inaugural class of Nu School, I received the title of 'Technology Fellow' where I would use my technology expertise to not only get hands-on in the software side of minimum viable product development, but also reach out to address technical issues, concerns, and comments on how to improve Nu School for upcoming years.

Curriculum

To enhance the experience of Nu School, multiple conferences were held by Y Combinator entrepreneurs that detailed the multiple aspects that should be considered when it comes to developing a startup. Nu School Fellows were expected to attend the conferences to aid them in their startup endeavors. Many conferences were held over the span of Nu School 2020, and they each were eye-opening. Below are just a few of the conferences that stood out the most to me

1

Product Development 101

Product Development 101 was a conference held by Luciano Arango, the CTO of PersistIQ. In this conference, Nu School Fellows had the oppurunity to witness firsthand some of the many ways to create a minimum viable product of their own to demonstrate during their pitches at the end of Nu School 2020.

2

Coding 101

 Taught by Helson Taveras, the Founder of General Computing, Coding 101 was a conference that detailed the best programming languages for specific tasks. Programming languages such as Python, JavaScript, and SQL were discussed for data analysis, website development, and database management respectively. Helson also guided us towards free resources to keep in mind when using programming to create products for a startup.  

3

Mental Health 101

Mental Health 101 was held by Bea Arthur, a licensed mental health counselor and CEO of Difference. Bea's conference emphasized the importance of caring for your mental health when running a startup. Topics covered were managing others, bouncing back emotionally when falling behind schedule on a project, and getting over a disastrous pitch.
