Why Wright?

What led to our team wanting to create Wright?

1.) Gen-Z is becoming increasingly aware of the sociopolitical landscape...

...and they're fired to voice their opinions! Don't believe us? Just look at Twitter, TikTok, Facebook, etc. Gen-Z, regardless of what their stances are, are eager to voice their opinions on current events. 

2.) Social Media has poor moderation! 

Viewing opinions on social media can be risky. There's very little accountability so people can often post whatever they want without much incentive to post the truth. (Fake news anyone?) Debates can also get a little too passionate, leading to hate comments, cyberbullying, etc. Their needs to be an outlet for Gen-Z to express their viewpoints in an articulate way!

3.) School is BORING

The only place Gen-Z has to articulate themselves in a safe enviroment is in school; particularly in graded school essays. The problem? The essays students are forced to write about in school are BORING. Instead of writing about issues currently impacting them, Gen-Z students are forced to argue in favor or against tiresome topics such as The Industrial Revolution, Moby-Dick, and everything in between.

4.) That's where Wright comes in!

Wright's goal is to provide users with new prompts every week dealing with current events that they can take a stance on and write in as many words as it takes to get their point across. Writers are expected to cite the sources in where they got their information from (akin to citing on Wikipedia) and readers can upvote well written responses, or downvote responses and annotate where they think mistakes are.
